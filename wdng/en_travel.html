How early in advance you book *Frankfurt (M) Flughafen is the train station connected to the Frankfurt airport ##Alex's sample itinerary ##Things to consider Dates are usually day/month/year instead of month/day/year (especially important when booking a train) Paying *Although some places will accept major credit cards, cash is more reliable. *Paying at a restaurant is a strange affair for an American. At the end of a meal a waiter will tell you how much your meal costs, and you calculate right then how much tip on top of that you would like to give. If paying in cash, you tell them how much change you want back (having accounted for your tip), and if paying in credit card you can just say "I would like to tip _ amount." Tip 10% at a restaurant for good service. Anywhere else (bars, cafes, etc) you can tip fifty cents to a dollar. Maybe American-sized tips will help assuage anti-Trump sentiment. *Most banks will charge you a fee to withdraw cash abroad, so keep that in mind. Make large purchases in credit card and smaller purchases in cash. You can check with your bank at home to see if they will do a free currency exchange for you before you leave the US - it's good to arrive already with a few hundred euros in hand. Jetlag *Try not to sleep until the evening, and get up before 10 the next day. It's easier if you get outside. *Learn from my mistakes, and when traveling the first day after arrival, set an alarm for yourself so that if you fall asleep you still won't miss getting on/off your train.
